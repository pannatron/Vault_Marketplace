# Product

## Register

product

## Users
Collectors and flippers of trading cards (Pokémon, sports, One Piece, MTG) plus crossover crypto-curious buyers. They arrive in two moods: the **hunt** (browsing graded cards and best deals for value) and the **thrill** (ripping a digital pack for the chance at a chase card). Mobile-heavy, impulse-driven, price-literate — they know FMV and spot a bad spread instantly.

## Product Purpose
Card Mania is a marketplace where physical, vaulted-and-insured trading cards trade as digital packs. Buy a pack, "rip" it, reveal a real graded card you own — keep it digital, trade it instantly, or redeem the physical. Success = the homepage converts a curious visitor into a first rip, and gives returning collectors a fast read on what's hot, what just pulled, and where the deals are.

## Brand Personality
Electric, collector-credible, a little obsessive. Three words: **thrill, trust, foil.** It should feel like the half-second before you flip the hit card under arcade light — energy without sketchiness. Numbers are honest (real FMV, real % off); the excitement is in the reveal, never in hiding the math.

## Anti-references
- **Courtyard.io's flat navy** — the reference, but we don't clone its muted corporate-dark palette; Card Mania is louder and more iridescent.
- **Generic dark SaaS dashboard** (Linear-clone gray-on-graphite) — too cold and tool-like for a hobby people love.
- **Casino / loot-box sleaze** — no slot-machine chrome, no fake-scarcity countdown pressure, no garish gold-everywhere. The thrill is earned by the cards, not manufactured anxiety.
- **NFT marketplace 2021** — no rainbow gradients on every surface, no jpeg-grid monotony.

## Design Principles
1. **The card is the hero.** Every surface exists to frame a card; chrome stays quiet so foil can glow.
2. **Honest thrill.** Show real FMV, real discounts, real pull times. Excitement comes from truth, not pressure tactics.
3. **Rarity is legible.** A collector should read common-vs-chase at a glance through a consistent rarity color language.
4. **Fast read, then deep dive.** Hot / just-pulled / deals scannable in seconds; detail one tap away.
5. **Familiar where it counts.** Buy buttons, qty steppers, search behave exactly as collectors expect — surprise lives in the reveal, not the controls.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text ≥4.5:1 on ink. Rarity never encoded by color alone — always paired with a label/tier name. Full `prefers-reduced-motion` path (foil shimmer + carousel motion become static/crossfade). Keyboard-navigable carousel, scroll rows, and qty steppers; visible focus rings.
